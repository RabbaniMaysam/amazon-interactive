Figures from the paper

Best-response phase portrait

Strategic complementarity of fees and self-preferencing

Both best responses slope upward, reflecting ∂2Π / ∂σ ∂τ > 0. Their intersection is the hybrid equilibrium.

Direct vs indirect welfare channels of self-preferencing

Self-preferencing harms welfare only through the fee

Dashed (direct, with τ held fixed at τ*): exactly flat, because the closed form W(τ) does not contain σ. Solid (indirect, along the platform's fee response): downward sloping because self-preferencing pulls τ up, and W is decreasing in τ.

Welfare-maximizing fee vs platform fee

Deadweight loss of the platform's privately optimal fee

Total surplus TS(τ) = W(τ) + Π(σ*(τ), τ). The welfare-maximizing τW sits below the platform's τ*. The shaded region between them is the deadweight loss.

Transitional ratchet of the platform footprint

A single path; λ controls speed only

Yt+1 = (1 − λ) Yt + λ Ye. Move the λ slider to see how the speed-of-adjustment parameter rescales the half-life of convergence while leaving the destination Yss unchanged.

Steady-state footprint comparative statics

Yss rises in δ, falls in K

The platform's long-run footprint is governed by patience δ and entry cost K. The λ-neutrality of the steady state is documented separately in the previous figure.

Cap cascade

Capping one instrument disciplines the other

A fee cap τ̄ below τ* shifts equilibrium to (σFC, τ̄) with σFC < σ*. A non-discrimination mandate σ=0 shifts equilibrium to (0, τND) along bτ.

Web-only constructions

Three further constructions that are clearer in three dimensions or as interactive overlays. These do not appear in the paper.

Profit surface Π(σ, τ)

3D view of the concave profit objective. Drag to rotate.

The platform's profit Π(σ, τ) as a surface. The red marker is the interior maximum (σ*, τ*). The local concavity around the peak verifies the second-order condition. Rendered in 3D because the geometry is clearer than a 2D contour map.

Combined regulation: welfare over (τ̄, σcap)

3D welfare surface over the policy space. Drag to rotate.

Consumer welfare W as a function of the fee cap τ̄ and the self-preferencing cap σcap. Three reference policies are marked. The combined cap (low τ̄, σcap=0) sits at the highest level of the surface; either single-instrument regime is dominated. The 3D surface makes the dominance more direct than a 2D heatmap.

Iso-NS versus welfare in (τ, F) space

Market-structure observables under-identify welfare

Heat color: consumer welfare W(τ, F) at fixed σ. The red curve is the locus of (τ, F) yielding the same active-seller count NS. Two points on the red curve generate the same market-structure observable but different consumer welfare. The value of the figure is in tracing the iso-NS curve across welfare contours interactively.
