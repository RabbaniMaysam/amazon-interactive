Figure 1. Best-response phase portrait

Strategic complementarity of fees and self-preferencing

Both best responses slope upward, reflecting ∂2Π / ∂σ ∂τ > 0. Their intersection is the hybrid equilibrium.

Profit surface Π(σ, τ) (web-only)

3D view of the concave profit objective. Drag to rotate.

The platform's profit Π(σ, τ) as a surface. The red marker is the interior maximum (σ*, τ*). The local concavity around the peak verifies the second-order condition. This figure is web-only because the 3D view conveys the geometry more cleanly than a 2D contour map.

Figure 3. Direct vs indirect welfare channels of self-preferencing

Self-preferencing harms welfare only through the fee

Top trace (with τ held fixed): the direct effect on W is exactly zero because the closed form W(τ) does not contain σ. Bottom trace (along the platform's fee response): downward sloping because self-preferencing pulls τ up, and W is decreasing in τ.

Figure 4. Welfare-maximizing fee vs platform fee

Deadweight loss of the platform's privately optimal fee

Total surplus TS(τ) = W(τ) + Π(σ*(τ), τ). The welfare-maximizing τW sits below the platform's τ*. The shaded region between them is the deadweight loss.

Figure 5. Transitional ratchet of the platform footprint

All paths converge to the same Yss

Yt+1 = (1 − λ) Yt + λ Ye. The speed-of-adjustment parameter λ controls the half-life of convergence but not the destination.

Figure 6. Steady-state footprint comparative statics

Yss rises in δ, falls in K, neutral in λ

The platform's long-run footprint is governed by patience δ and entry cost K. The entry-speed parameter λ affects only the transition, not the steady state.

Figure 7. Cap cascade

Capping one instrument disciplines the other

A fee cap τ̄ below τ* shifts equilibrium to (σFC, τ̄) with σFC < σ*. A non-discrimination mandate σ=0 shifts equilibrium to (0, τND) along bτ.

Combined regulation: welfare over (τ̄, σcap) (web-only)

3D welfare surface over the policy space. Drag to rotate.

Consumer welfare W as a function of the fee cap τ̄ and the self-preferencing cap σcap. Three reference policies are marked. The combined cap (low τ̄, σcap=0) sits at the highest level of the surface; either single-instrument regime is dominated. Web-only because the 3D view of the welfare surface conveys the dominance more directly than a 2D heatmap.

Iso-NS versus welfare in (τ, F) space (web-only)

Market-structure observables under-identify welfare

Heat color: consumer welfare W(τ, F) at fixed σ. The red curve is the locus of (τ, F) yielding the same active-seller count NS. Two points on the red curve generate the same market-structure observable but different consumer welfare. Web-only because the value of the figure is in tracing the iso-NS curve across welfare contours interactively.
